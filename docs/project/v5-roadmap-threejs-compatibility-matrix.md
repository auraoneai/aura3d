# V5 Three.js Compatibility Matrix

> Historical note: This V5 document is retained as project history after the V9 parity reset. Current planning, claim boundaries, and code-backed parity status live in `docs/project/v9-roadmap-status.md`, `docs/project/v9-roadmap-parity-matrix.md`, and `docs/project/v9-roadmap-three-js-parity-plan.md`. Treat unchecked tasks or old claims here as historical unless they are restated in the V9 docs.


The V5 compatibility matrix is generated by:

```sh
pnpm v5:threejs-inventory
```

Generated artifacts:

- `tests/reports/v5-threejs-inventory.json`
- `tests/reports/v5-threejs-compatibility-matrix.json`

Release thresholds:

- Overall supported or partial coverage must reach at least 60%.
- Core, math, cameras, lights, materials, geometries, and textures must reach at least 80% supported or partial coverage.
- Controls, loaders, postprocessing, animation, and helpers must reach at least 60% supported or partial coverage.
- At least 250 Three.js API/example entries must be tracked.

At Milestone 1, the matrix defines the target and tracks current status. Later milestones must move entries from `planned` or `blocked` to `partial` or `supported` only when tests, browser evidence, docs, and migration proof exist.
