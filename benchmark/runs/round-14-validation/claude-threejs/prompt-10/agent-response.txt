## What I built

A Three.js product viewer in `source/src/main.ts` (the only file I changed — the rest is the runner's fixed Vite/TypeScript scaffold). It uses only the bundled `three` (0.165.0) and its addons (`OrbitControls`, `GLTFLoader`), plus the provided `sneaker.glb`.

Requirements covered:
- **Model loaded & visible** — `GLTFLoader` loads `/benchmark/assets/sneaker.glb` (the real scaffold path under `public/`; no invented path).
- **Centered & auto-scaled** — computes the bounding box, recenters at origin, and uniformly scales so the largest dimension is 2 world units, then lifts the base to rest on the plinth top.
- **Plinth/base** — a cylindrical plinth with its top at y=0, plus a shadow-catching ground plane.
- **Studio lighting** — hemisphere + ambient base with a three-point setup (shadow-casting key, fill, rim), ACES tone mapping, sRGB output, and soft shadows for readable material.
- **Orbit controls** — `OrbitControls` with damping, distance/polar limits, targeted at the product.
- **Turntable** — the model is parented to a pivot group spinning at 6 RPM in the `setAnimationLoop`.
