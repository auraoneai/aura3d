The neon tunnel flythrough is complete, builds cleanly, and renders correctly (verified via headless screenshots, no console errors).

## What was built

A procedural neon tunnel flythrough in `source/src/main.ts` (Three.js 0.165.0 + Vite + TypeScript):

- **Procedural tube geometry** — a closed winding `CatmullRomCurve3` path drives a `TubeGeometry` (600 tubular segments), rendered `BackSide` so the camera sees the tunnel interior.
- **Emissive segments** — 90 bright `TorusGeometry` ring "gates" placed and tangent-aligned along the curve (pulsing cyan/magenta/blue/pink), plus a procedurally-generated canvas `emissiveMap` of glowing ring bands and longitudinal neon strips on the tube wall.
- **Bloom postprocess** — `EffectComposer` → `RenderPass` → `UnrealBloomPass` (threshold 0.45 so only neon blooms) → `OutputPass` (ACES tone mapping + sRGB).
- **Fog falloff** — `FogExp2` (density 0.045) fades distant segments into the dark for depth.
- **Camera animation** — the camera flies along the curve each frame, looking slightly ahead with a gentle roll; travelling colored point lights catch the metallic wall. Loops seamlessly.

I added `@types/three@0.165.0` to `source/package.json` devDependencies (needed for the TypeScript `typecheck` step). No files outside `source/` were modified.
