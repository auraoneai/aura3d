## Summary

I built an animated primitive humanoid in `source/src/main.ts` using Three.js (the version pinned in the scaffold). The figure is a hierarchy of pivot groups so each joint animates independently:

- **Head** — `SphereGeometry` (plus a small sphere "nose" to give a clear facing direction)
- **Torso** — `CylinderGeometry` (tapered), with a `CylinderGeometry` pelvis
- **Arms & legs** — `BoxGeometry` limb segments (upper arm/forearm, thigh/shin) jointed at shoulder/elbow and hip/knee, with box hands and feet
- **Ground** — a `PlaneGeometry` that receives shadows, with a `GridHelper` overlay so the crossing motion reads clearly

**Walk cycle (fully procedural, no assets):** hips swing in opposite phase, knees flex (positive-only) through each lift, arms counter-swing to the legs with relaxed elbow bend, the torso counter-rotates and side-sways, and the whole body bobs vertically at twice the stride frequency. The character walks a circular path across the plane, always facing its direction of travel — so it's continuously crossing the ground and any captured frame shows a dynamic mid-stride pose. Shadows, hemisphere + directional lighting, fog, and OrbitControls round it out.
