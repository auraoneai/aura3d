## Summary

Built a Three.js product viewer for the provided `sneaker.glb` in the clean source directory (`source/`). The build passed (`tsc --noEmit` + `vite build`).

**What it does** (all in `source/src/main.ts`):
- **Loads the sneaker** via `GLTFLoader` from `/benchmark/assets/sneaker.glb` — the real served path of the scaffold-placed asset at `source/public/benchmark/assets/sneaker.glb`. No invented or substitute paths.
- **Centered & auto-scaled**: computes a `Box3`, scales the longest dimension to a fixed target, then re-centers on X/Z and rests the base flush on the plinth top.
- **Plinth/product base**: a cylinder plinth plus a floor disc that receives contact shadows.
- **Studio lighting**: `RoomEnvironment` IBL via `PMREMGenerator` for readable PBR reflections, plus a three-point directional rig (key with shadows, fill, rim) and ambient fill, with ACES filmic tone mapping and sRGB output.
- **Orbit controls**: `OrbitControls` with damping, distance/polar limits, targeted at the product.
- **Turntable rotation**: model + plinth sit in a `Group` rotated continuously (delta-time based) in the `setAnimationLoop`, independent of user orbiting.

**Note on the scaffold**: Three.js 0.165.0 ships no TypeScript declarations and the fixed scaffold includes no `@types/three`, so I added a self-contained ambient module shim (`source/src/three-shims.d.ts`) so the strict typecheck passes. No files outside `source/` were edited.
