## Summary

Built an animated primitive humanoid in raw Three.js (0.165.0) at `source/src/main.ts`.

**What it does**
- **Humanoid from primitives only**: sphere head (+ small cylinder neck), tapered **cylinder torso**, **box** pelvis, arms, and legs, with small box hands/feet — proportioned to read clearly as a humanoid.
- **Procedural walk-cycle**: each limb hangs from a pivot group at its joint and swings via `sin(t)` — legs and arms in opposite phase — plus a 2×-frequency vertical body bob and slight torso counter-rotation so any captured frame shows a dynamic mid-stride pose (not a static stick figure).
- **Moves across the ground**: the figure traverses a large circular path (`setAnimationLoop`), facing its direction of travel, so it's always traversing the ground plane and stays in frame for runtime evidence.
- **Visible ground plane**: 80×80 lit `MeshStandardMaterial` plane with a grid overlay; directional + hemisphere lights with soft shadows.
